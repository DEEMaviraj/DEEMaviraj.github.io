Maze of UCSC

C Game Development 3-Floor Maze Multi-Player File I/O

Overview

Maze of UCSC is a classical three player maze runner game developed in C. Players navigate a three floor maze using dice rolls to capture a randomly placed flag. The maze features stairs connecting floors bidirectionally, poles for rapid descent, impassable walls, and a special zone called 'Bawana' that applies random status effects to any player who enters. All maze elements, walls, stairs, poles, and the flag are loaded from plain text configuration files, making the game fully customisable without recompiling.

Key Rules

Rule 1 — Entry

Players must roll a 6 on the movement dice to enter the maze from the starting area.

Rule 2 — Movement

Two dice: movement (1-6) and direction. Direction dice is thrown every 4th roll. No partial movement is allowed.

Rule 4 — Stairs & Poles

Landing mid-movement on a stair or pole triggers it. Remaining moves continue from the new landing cell.

Rule 5 — Capture

Landing on a cell occupied by another player sends that player back to their starting area.

Rule 6 — Stair Flip

Every 5 rounds, stair directions randomly reverse , previously up-only stairs may become down-only.

Rule 7 — Bawana

Special zone with 12 cells giving: Food Poisoning, Disoriented, Triggered, Happy, or bonus movement points.

Rule 8 — Consumables

Each cell has a consumable value (0-4) deducted from movement points as the player passes through it.

Rule 11 — Depletion

If movement points reach zero or go negative, the player is immediately transported to Bawana.

Bawana Effects

Food Poisoning

Player misses 3 full rounds and is randomly repositioned within Bawana after recovery with zero movement points.

Disoriented

+50 movement points. Player moves randomly for 4 throws , direction dice is fully overridden.

Triggered

+50 movement points. Player moves at double speed ,a dice roll of 4 moves the player 8 cells.

Happy

+200 movement points. Placed at the Bawana entrance with no negative side effects.

Input Files

stairs.txt — Stair connections between floors, e.g. [0, 4, 5, 2, 0, 10]
poles.txt — Sliding pole positions and floor range, e.g. [0, 2, 5, 24]
walls.txt — Impassable wall segments per floor, e.g. [1, 0, 2, 8, 2]
flag.txt — Single flag location, e.g. [floor, width, length]
seed.txt — RNG seed for reproducible and testable game sessions
